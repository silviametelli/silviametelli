About.

I am an Assistant Professor (fixed term) at the University of Paris and a researcher at the Research Center in Epidemiology and Statistics Université de Paris, CRESS-U1153. Before that, I was a Research Associate at The Alan Turing Institute and Imperial College London, under the Grand Challenge "Monitoring Complex Systems with Rare High Consequence Events".

My main research interests are network meta-analysis and living network meta-analysis for meta-research and dynamic network approaches for the study of multimorbidity progression in chronic diseases. I am also involved in statistical methods for dynamic treatment regimes in personalised medicine.

More specifically, I work on:

  • multivariate mixed models for meta-research
  • nonparametric latent feature models
  • dimensionality reduction/clustering
  • computational Bayesian inference
