Bio.

I am a Marie Skłodowska-Curie Individual Fellow at Université de Paris, and part of the INSERM Research Center of Epidemiology and Statistics. Before that, I was a postdoctoral Research Associate at The Alan Turing Institute and Imperial College London.

I am interested in statistical methods for personalised digital health, dynamic networks and meta-analysis. Due to my PhD studies, I also enjoy to work on anomaly-detection for large computer/communication networks.

More specifically, I work on:

  • computational Bayesian inference
  • Bayesian nonparametrics
  • dimensionality reduction and clustering
  • marked temporal point processes


Education.

MRes in Mathematical Sciences, Imperial College London
BSc, MSc in Statistical Sciences, University of Florence
